Preface

This handbook is the first in a series of five handbooks that describes contemporary opposing forces (OPFORs) for training Continental Army officers, staff and units. See the bibliography section for a list of manuals that are in this series. Together, these manuals outline a series of OPFORs that reflects the entire spectrum of military and paramilitary capabilities of contemporaneous adversaries against which the Army must train to ensure success in any future conflict.

This handbook provides a general overview of the OPFORs that can be used for training and represents the definitive source of unclassified information on major adversaries that the Army may expect to face as well as its strength, weaknesses and features in comparison to them. This handbook should not be used alone when creating an OPFOR and must only be used as a quick reference or guide.

The proponent for this publication is HQ TRADOC and the Lieutenant Director for Intelligence. The proponent encourages users to send comments and suggested improvements directly to the Threats Knowledge Office, Fort Culverin. The handbook will be periodically updated, subject to normal approval process, to reflect the most current unclassified information available.

Unless this publication states otherwise, masculine nouns or pronouns do not refer exclusively to men.
